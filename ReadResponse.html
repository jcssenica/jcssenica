Response to Critical Atlas of the Internet

Jessica Chen


Louise Durlhe’s Critical Atlas of the Internet is crafted and delivered beautifully and poetically with written words and digital website layouts. I really enjoyed this type of delivery of text and the flow it creates.


I am most attracted and inspired by the word Architecture: “Internet architecture is controlled by search engines,” “There are many Internet architectures as there are users.” Never have I thought about comparing the internet to architecture, however, it is an effective metaphor that shows the building, the development, and endless progressing traits of the internet. This website strengthened my idea of the structure of the internet, knowing that there are a “base”, “layers (hidden layers)”, “branches”, “individual users” to form the internet, just like in architecture there are fundamental structures, levels of buildings, elevators and platforms, and individual units or cells. “Internet is a mirror reflecting our terrestrial position” This sentence is so poetic and I loved it. In the earthspace, people are distinctively, purposefully, and being controlled in areas that are fenced by multiple reasons, regionally, socially, and politically. As described in the article/the website, the internet is also structured like geographical settlements. However, as the “Search engines continually organize and reorganize the web”, the separations, connections, and placements of the web structure became ambiguous and the human space placements can hardly be compared with it. As much as this quote sounds natural, it also implies that human are using their powers to modify the organization of the web in both positive and negative ways. This ambiguous quote leads to another quote, "In order to exist, cyberspace needs equipment and a concrete infrastructure in terrestrial space. With this physical equipment, the Internet and cyberspace remain solidly anchored in geographical space. The Internet is a virtual network built into physical reality. It’s a cloud that puts down roots into concrete." It is interesting to think about how humans and the algorithm are interplaying in this building of the internet, with intentional and unintentional purposes.


“The difference between earthspace and cyberspace: On Earth, people move towards their centers of interest; on the Internet, we are at the center and the space is created around us”
How far away is specific information from where I am on the internet? For instance, how far away is news about the stock market away from me as a user of the search engine who typically, merely searches anything about design, fashion, or shopping sites. Maybe I will encounter it after a thousand times of searching? Maybe a million times of searching? It’s frightening to think that the information could be right beside you (right beside the thing you searched for on the search engine), but you could never come across it because of the algorithm and this user-centered design aspect. Following this logic, the internet brings people apart rather than shortening the distance between individuals, since every search engine user will stay in their own bubble created by their own interest with the help of the internet. But is it really encouraged? One of the goals of developing technology and the internet is to bring people closer and make this traveling of digital and physical distance easier. However, with this personalized algorithm and user-centered experience design, I resonate with people who are getting lonelier on the internet.
